## 🔹 **1. Sources of Law in India (Kahan se kanoon banta hai?)**

### 1.1 **Primary Legislation** – *Directly made by Parliament/States*

* **Acts/Laws** banaye jaate hain MPs/MLAs se.
* 🧾 *Example:*

  * **IPC (1860)** – Crimes ke rules
  * **GST Act (2017)** – Tax system ka law

### **Subsidiary / Delegated Legislation** – *Authorities banaate hain chhoti chhoti rules*

* Jaise RBI ya SEBI jo rules banaate hain banks ya share market ke liye.
* Parliament unko power deta hai detailed rules banane ki.

---

### 1.2 **Case Law / Common Law** – *Judges ke diye gaye decisions*

* **Doctrine of Stare Decisis** – *“Pehle jo decide hua, wahi follow karo”*
* **Article 141**: Supreme Court ke judgments sab courts ko follow karne padte hain.
* **Foreign judgments** – UK/US ke cases sirf *inspiration* hote hain, binding nahi.

🔹 **Famous Examples:**

* *Kesavananda Bharati (1973)* – Constitution ka *Basic Structure* badla nahi jaa sakta.
* *Maneka Gandhi Case (1978)* – *Right to Life* ka matlab sirf zinda rehna nahi, dignity bhi.

---

### 1.3 **Customary & Religious Laws**

* **Hindu Law**: Jaise *Hindu Succession Act*
* **Muslim Law**: Jaise *Shariat*
* **Tribal Laws**: Jaise Nagaland ke *customs* – unka apna local justice system hota hai.

---

### 1.4 **Constitutional Law** – *Sabse upar Constitution hai*

* Constitution = *Mother Law*
* Har law ko iske rules ke andar rehna padta hai.
* Agar koi law Constitution ke against hua → **Invalid** ho jaata hai.

---

## 🔹 **2. Court Structure in India (Nyay Vyavastha)**

### 2.1 **Supreme Court (Topmost Court)**

📍 *Delhi mein hai*

* **Original Jurisdiction (Art. 131)**: Centre vs State ka jhagda
* **Appellate Jurisdiction**: High Court ke decision ke against appeal
* **Writs (Art. 32)**: Fundamental Rights protect karta hai. *Direct SC ja sakte ho*

---

### 2.2 **High Courts**

📍 *Har state ya states group ka ek HC*

* 25 HCs hain India mein. (e.g. Bombay HC → Maharashtra & Goa)
* **Writs (Art. 226)** – SC se bhi zyada powerful, *Fundamental + Legal Rights* ke liye
* **Art. 227** – Niche courts ko control karta hai

---

### 2.3 **Subordinate Courts (Zila level ke courts)**

| 🏛 Court                            | 🔍 Function                                                  |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **District & Sessions Court**       | Sabse bada court in a district (civil + criminal dono cases) |
| **Chief Judicial Magistrate (CJM)** | Serious criminal matters                                     |
| **Judicial Magistrate (JM)**        | Chhote-chhote cases (theft, minor fights)                    |
| **Lok Adalat**                      | Fast aur friendly court – out-of-court settlement            |

---

### ⚖ Specialized Tribunals:

* **NCLT** – Company cases
* **CAT** – Govt job related disputes
* **ITAT** – Tax related appeals

---

💡 **Summary:**

* Laws 4 jagah se aate hain: Parliament, Rules, Court Judgments, Religion/Customs
* Courts 3 levels: SC → HC → Local Courts
* Constitution is king 👑 – sab laws uske under hi valid hote hain.

## 🔹 **3. Key Legal Doctrines (Important Legal Principles)**

### 3.1 **Rule of Law**

📜 *"Sab kanoon ke saamne barabar hain"*

* **Art. 14**: Chahe PM ho ya aam aadmi – sabke liye same law

### 3.2 **Judicial Review**

👨‍⚖ *Court check karega – koi law Constitution ke khilaaf toh nahi?*

* **Art. 13, 32, 226** → SC/HC kisi bhi unconstitutional law ko **null** kar sakte hain.

### 3.3 **PIL (Public Interest Litigation)**

🧑‍🌾👩‍⚖ *Public ke fayde ke liye case file kar sakte ho, bina khud affected hone ke bhi!*

* Example: Environment, child labor, air pollution, etc.

## 🔹 **4. Common Misconceptions (Galatfehmiyaan)**

❌ *"Customary laws sab par haavi hote hain."*
✅ **Reality**: Custom tabhi chalega jab **statutory law** ke khilaaf na ho.
👉 *Example:* Triple Talaq banned in **Shayara Bano Case (2017)**

---

❌ *"Lok Adalat judgement jabardasti hoti hai."*
✅ **Reality**: Sirf **mutual agreement** se settlement hoti hai.
👉 No agreement = No verdict!

## 🏛️ 1. Indian Court Hierarchy

### 🔹 **Subordinate Courts (District Level)**

* **District & Sessions Court**: District level ka highest court hota hai. Civil cases mein "District Judge" aur criminal cases mein "Sessions Judge" hota hai. Ye serious matters jaise murder, rape, etc., ko handle karta hai.

* **Magistrate Courts**:

  * **Chief Judicial Magistrate (CJM)**: Serious criminal offenses ko handle karta hai, jaise dacoity, kidnapping.
  * **Judicial Magistrate (JM)**: Chhoti crimes jaise theft, assault ko handle karta hai.

* **District Consumer Forum**: Agar kisi product ya service mein dikkat hai aur uski value ₹1 crore se kam hai, toh yahan complaint kar sakte ho.

### 🔹 **High Courts (State Level)**

* Har state ka apna High Court hota hai. Ye lower courts ke decisions ke against appeals sunta hai.
* Agar kisi ka fundamental right violate ho raha hai, toh Article 226 ke under writ petition file kar sakte ho.

### 🔹 **Supreme Court (National Level)**

* Ye desh ka highest court hai.
* Agar Centre aur State ke beech koi dispute ho, toh Article 131 ke under Supreme Court decide karta hai.
* Agar kisi High Court ke decision se khush nahi ho, toh yahan appeal kar sakte ho.([icamindia][1])

## ⚖️ 2. Arbitration (Alternative Dispute Resolution)

### 🔹 **Kya hai Arbitration?**

Jab do parties court ke bahar apne disputes ko solve karna chahti hain, toh wo arbitration choose karti hain. Ye private process hota hai jahan neutral arbitrator decision leta hai.

### 🔹 **Process:**

1. **Arbitration Agreement**: Dono parties likhit mein agree karti hain ki dispute arbitration se solve hoga.
2. **Arbitrator Appoint Karna**: Dono parties milke ek ya teen arbitrators appoint karti hain.
3. **Hearing & Evidence**: Dono sides apne arguments aur evidence present karti hain.
4. **Award**: Arbitrator decision deta hai, jo legally binding hota hai.

### 🔹 **Advantages:**

* **Faster Resolution**: Court cases mein saal lagte hain, arbitration mein kuch mahine.
* **Confidentiality**: Sab kuch private hota hai, public record nahi banta.
* **Expert Arbitrators**: Parties apne field ke expert ko arbitrator bana sakti hain.
* **Cost-Effective**: Court ke mukable mein kam kharcha hota hai.

## 📚 3. Key Areas of Law

### 🔹 **Contract Law**

* Agar do log ya companies ke beech agreement hota hai aur koi party usse follow nahi karti, toh ye breach of contract hota hai.
* **Example**: Agar tu kisi se website banwane ka contract karta hai aur wo time par deliver nahi karta, toh tu uske against case kar sakta hai.

### 🔹 **Tort Law**

* Jab kisi ki negligence se dusre ko nuksan hota hai, toh wo tort law ke under aata hai.
* **Example**: Agar koi doctor galat treatment deta hai jisse patient ko nuksan hota hai, toh patient compensation claim kar sakta hai.

### 🔹 **Workplace Law**

* **Industrial Disputes Act, 1947**: Agar koi employee unfairly terminate hota hai, toh ye act uski madad karta hai.
* **POSH Act, 2013**: Workplace mein sexual harassment se protect karta hai.
* **Minimum Wages Act, 1948**: Ensure karta hai ki employees ko minimum wages milein.([Wikipedia][2])

## 🌍 4. Foreign Court Systems Comparison

### 🔹 **United States**

* **Court Structure**: District Courts → Circuit Courts → Supreme Court.
* **Jury Trials**: Zyada tar cases mein jury decide karti hai.

### 🔹 **United Kingdom**

* **Court Structure**: Magistrates' Courts → Crown Courts → Court of Appeal → Supreme Court.
* **Tribunals**: Employment, immigration jaise matters ke liye specialized tribunals hote hain.

### 🔹 **Comparison with India**

* India mein jury system nahi hai, judges hi decision lete hain.
* India mein bhi specialized tribunals hote hain jaise NCLT, CAT.
